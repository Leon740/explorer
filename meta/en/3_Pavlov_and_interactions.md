# Pavlov and interactions

There was a scientist named Pavlov. He worked in neurophysiology and other sciences. He conducted many experiments, and here is one of them. Before feeding, a signal was given to a dog. The dog began to salivate even before the food appeared. If the signal is changed, the dog adapts to the new one.

A dog is, of course, not a human, but people do something similar.

People often perceive events as one-off occurrences. However, after each event or interaction, both participants and observers draw conclusions, form expectations, and so on. That is, after every interaction with you, people will either be more likely to reach out to you and recommend you - or to avoid you.

Example: a colleague comes to you with a difficult task.  
You solve it. Most likely, both that colleague and the observers will come to you with similar tasks again.

How I apply this: Interactions do not have to be easy or soft. Escalation and confrontation are necessary. But if you value, respect, and genuinely care about the people around you, you interact in a “pleasant way”. I would even say that you attract similar kinds of tasks and through them, similar kinds of people.
